# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

VoxFlow aims to be a respectful, practical project for users and contributors of all backgrounds and experience levels. Discussions should stay constructive, technically grounded, and safe for collaboration.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and professional in issues, pull requests, reviews, and other project spaces.
- Focus on the code, design, data, and reproducible behavior rather than on individuals.
- Give clear technical feedback with context, evidence, and actionable suggestions.
- Assume good intent, but ask for clarification when something is ambiguous.
- Respect privacy and confidentiality, especially when discussing user audio, transcripts, logs, or security-sensitive details.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, discrimination, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Trolling, baiting, bad-faith arguments, or deliberately disruptive behavior.
- Publishing someone else's private information, credentials, proprietary data, or unredacted recordings without permission.
- Spamming, derailing technical threads, or repeatedly ignoring maintainer guidance.
- Publicly posting exploit details or sensitive security information before maintainers have had a chance to assess it.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to repository spaces including issues, pull requests, reviews, and release-related discussions. It also applies to off-platform interactions when someone is representing this project or its maintainers.

## Reporting and Enforcement

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, contact the maintainers privately through GitHub. If the report includes a security or privacy concern, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) and avoid posting sensitive details publicly.

Maintainers may take any action they believe is appropriate, including:

- editing, hiding, or removing content
- requesting changes in tone or behavior
- temporarily restricting participation
- permanently removing a contributor from project spaces

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will generally respond in proportion to the behavior involved:

- Correction: a private or public note describing the problem and the expected adjustment
- Warning: a clear notice that repeated behavior will lead to stronger action
- Temporary restriction: a short-term loss of participation privileges
- Permanent restriction: removal from the project's collaborative spaces
